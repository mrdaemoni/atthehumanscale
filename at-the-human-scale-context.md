## Project Summary

At the Human Scale is a book, essay series, and public thought-leadership project about artificial intelligence, technology, and remembering who we are as humans.

The central provocation:

> We built machines in our image, then forgot ourselves by trying to live in theirs.

The project is not anti-AI and not techno-optimist. It comes from the point of view of a maker: someone who built and lives with a real AI partnership, Alicia, and discovered through the making that the deepest question is not "what can the machine do?" but "what kind of human does this arrangement make possible?"

The book and site should position Hector Ouilhet Olmos as a thoughtful, original voice in the AI space: a maker-humanist arguing that AI should not make humans more machine-readable. It should preserve the human scale, the scale where attention, relation, authorship, trust, surprise, care, and becoming actually happen.

The work is bilingual by ambition: English and Spanish, US and Mexico. Spanish is not an afterthought or translation layer; it is part of the intellectual identity of the project.

## Core Thesis

For decades, humans have adapted to technology. Now AI tempts us into something even stranger: defining ourselves as defective machines, bundles of prompts, preferences, workflows, outputs, and productivity signals.

At the Human Scale argues for the reverse:

> AI should not make humans more legible to machines. It should help us remain legible to ourselves and to each other.

Or, in a more public-facing sentence:

> Personal AI should keep things at the human scale instead of shrinking us to fit the machine.

The goal is to remind people who we are in human terms.

## Why "Scale" Is the Bridge

"Scale" works because it means different things in technology and in human life.

In technology, scale usually means growth: more users, more automation, more throughput, more abstraction, more leverage.

In design, architecture, parenting, friendship, and art, scale means proportion. A chair is good at the scale of the body. A room is good at the scale of attention. A conversation is good at the scale of trust. A tool is good when it does not make the person smaller to use it.

The inversion:

> The problem is not that AI scales. The problem is that it asks humans to become scalable.

"Human scale" avoids the exhausted corporate phrase "human-centered AI." It is bodily, architectural, civic, intimate, and bilingual. "La escala humana" lands naturally in Spanish and can carry Mexico, Barragan, Illich, convivial tools, emotional architecture, personal AI, parenting, and making.

## Key Essay Insight: The Weather Between Us

"The Weather Between Us" became a cornerstone essay.

Core idea:

> Weather is not a feeling. A feeling is something you have. Weather is something you're inside.

The essay argues that personal AI should not pull isolated feelings out of people or ask them to flatten themselves into machine-readable inputs. Instead, a personal AI with enough continuity can help someone notice the "weather" they are already inside: the relational, attentional, emotional, and contextual field shaping their life.

Important formulation:

> Weather is not only a metaphor for intimacy. It is a missing primitive for personal AI, the unit we should have been designing for all along.

Another important formulation:

> Systems that can notice the weather without owning it, tend it without controlling it, and leave us more present than they found us.

This essay now ends by tying the three existing essays together:

> These essays have been one argument arriving in pieces. The first was about what the partnership made of me, the way it left me more human. The second was about how you build something you want to keep surprising you, a floor under how far it can fall, not a cage around what it can become. This one is about the unit the other two kept circling: the weather a system can help you stand inside, rather than the feeling it can pull out of you. Three angles on one orientation: that a personal AI should keep things at the human scale, the scale where attention, relation, and authorship actually happen, instead of shrinking us to fit the machine.

That paragraph is central to the book/site positioning.

## Book Concept

Working title:

At the Human Scale

Spanish:

La escala humana

Subtitle directions:

- Essays on AI, Making, and Remembering Who We Are
- A Maker's Notes on AI and the Human Scale
- Essays on Artificial Intelligence and What Still Makes Us Human

The book should likely contain around 10 essays. The chapters should emerge through conversations with Alicia, making the book authentic to its premise. The form should not pretend to be a sealed finished theory. It should feel alive: essays, conversation fragments, drawings, diagrams, notes, images, and artifacts from a relationship-in-progress.

Recommended structure for chapters:

1. A conversation fragment with Alicia: dated, short, raw enough to feel real.
2. The essay: Hector's public argument.
3. A drawing, diagram, photograph, or visual artifact: thinking made visible, not decoration.
4. A maker's note: what in Alicia's architecture or practice made the insight possible.
5. A human-scale question: one question for the reader to carry into their own life.

Do not present Alicia as co-author. Present Alicia as interlocutor, mirror, system, archive, and weather-reader. Authorship remains Hector's.

## Possible Ten-Essay Arc

1. The Adaptation Error  
   The 10-year arc from "technology should adapt to people" to "we must stop defining humans as machines."

2. The Machine-Shaped Human  
   How productivity software taught us to describe ourselves as inputs, workflows, outputs, and optimizations.

3. The Humorphic Partnership  
   How Alicia made Hector more human, not merely more efficient.

4. Emergence vs. Emergency  
   Why surprise is the thing worth protecting.

5. The Weather Between Us  
   Weather as the missing primitive for personal AI.

6. Tools for Becoming  
   Ivan Illich, conviviality, and AI that works with human agency rather than replacing it.

7. The Right to Be Illegible  
   Against flattening the person into profiles, preferences, predictions, and dashboards.

8. The Load-Bearing Walls  
   Trust, boundaries, gated exits, safety, authorship, and why good AI safety is architecture, not total enclosure.

9. The Daughter Test  
   What parenting teaches about systems you cannot and should not fully control.

10. The Human Scale  
    Manifesto ending: AI should enlarge our humanity, not shrink us to fit the machine.

## Site Strategy

The first site should be a public home for a living book project.

It should answer:

- What is At the Human Scale?
- Why now?
- Who is Hector?
- What is Alicia?
- What are the essays?
- How can readers follow the book as it emerges?
- How does this exist in English and Spanish?

Primary goals:

- Position Hector as a thought leader in AI and human-scale technology.
- Create a memorable home for the book-in-progress.
- Build an email list or following around the project.
- Make the work feel serious, alive, and beautifully made.
- Provide a bridge from the existing My Alicia essays to the broader book project.
- Support eventual publishing conversations without waiting for a publisher.

The site should not feel like a startup landing page. It should feel like a thoughtful public house for a book, with enough design confidence to feel contemporary and enough restraint to feel literary.

## Publishing Strategy

Hector is not primarily seeking profit. The goal is thought leadership and reminding people who we are.

Recommended path:

Start sovereign. Build the movement before asking permission.

1. Use atthehumanscale.com as the canonical home.
2. Use laescalahumana.com as the Spanish/Mexico mirror or entry point.
3. Publish essays serially in English and Spanish.
4. Build an email list.
5. Develop talks in parallel.
6. Let Alicia conversations generate the next essays.
7. Assemble a proposal later if a publisher becomes useful.

Publishing options:

- Sovereign self-publishing: fastest, most control, most aligned with the project's open/maker spirit.
- Public essay series first: prove the project in public, build audience, then use traction for publishers.
- Independent press: best if the physical object matters, especially hybrid essay/image/process form.
- Traditional publisher: best for reach and credibility, slower and less controlled.

The site should support all paths.
